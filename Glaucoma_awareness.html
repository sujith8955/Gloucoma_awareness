Understanding Glaucoma

What is Glaucoma?

Glaucoma is a group of eye conditions that damage the optic nerve, typically due to high pressure in the eye.

Recognizing Glaucoma

Symptoms of glaucoma may include blurred vision, halos around lights, severe eye pain, nausea, and vomiting. However, in most cases, glaucoma develops slowly and without noticeable symptoms.

Self-Care for Glaucoma

If you have glaucoma, it's important to follow your doctor's recommendations, which may include using prescribed eye drops, attending regular check-ups, and maintaining a healthy lifestyle.
